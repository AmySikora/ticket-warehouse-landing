Marketing & Go-To-Market Plan

Our 3–6 month strategy for launching Ticket Warehouse — reaching early adopters, partners, and investors while building credibility in the ticketing ecosystem.

Executive Overview

Ticket Warehouse prevents duplicate ticket sales by synchronizing inventory across marketplaces in real time. Our marketing plan focuses on visibility, trust, and early adoption — positioning Ticket Warehouse as the first reliable, developer-friendly sync solution for ticket brokers, venues, and marketplaces.

Target Audiences

  • Independent Ticket Brokers: Simplify multi-platform management and prevent oversells.
  • Venues & Promoters: Gain real-time control of verified inventory and reporting.
  • Marketplaces: Integrate to ensure data consistency and protect buyers.

Strategy Phases

Phase 1: Awareness (Month 1–2)

  • Launch website and share origin story on LinkedIn and tech communities.
  • Reach out to small brokers for user interviews and beta testing.
  • Create educational content about API reliability and integration ease.

Phase 2: Engagement (Month 3–4)

  • Release developer documentation and demo videos.
  • Publish case studies showing time saved and reduced refund rates.
  • Collect testimonials from early adopters.

Phase 3: Growth (Month 5–6)

  • Pitch to startup incubators and ticketing networks for funding or pilots.
  • Build brand trust through partnerships with verified resellers.
  • Expand outreach to venues and small-ticket aggregators.

Funding & Growth

We plan to raise an initial $25,000 to support hosting, API scaling, and marketing outreach. Funds will go toward:

  • Cloud infrastructure for scalable API hosting
  • Marketing and partner outreach campaigns
  • Legal and incorporation setup
  • Developer time for dashboard and integration features
